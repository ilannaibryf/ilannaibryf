Recent Work

Design Engineer for UChicago Hospital - MR Elastography

Collaborated with Dr. Daniel Ginat, Assistant Professor of Radiology and Director of Head and Neck Imaging at the University of Chicago Hospital, in creating a passive driver for Magnetic Resonance Elastography imaging of the head and neck regions.

Radiologists need a passive driver tailored to the head and neck regions that generates accurate results via MRE so that useful health information about the head and neck can be obtained more efficiently,  less invasively, and with reduced costs relative to currently available technologies.

Voltaic Systems: Prototype Engineer

I conducted quality assurance and control testing on custom solar panels for industrial applications. I also designed and integrated solar to IoT applications:

1. Engineered an Autonomous Solar-Powered Raspberry Pi Camera with cellular connectivity for remote monitoring. Documented it entireley in a post I created a post to demonstrate how you can set up and program your own Raspberry Pi in this way.

2. Set up and implemented a solar-powered LoRa/LoRaWAN communication gateway in the Brooklyn Navy Yard to create a network of low power, low payload nodes with internet connection without using a cellular modem or WiFi.

Independent research: Bionic Prosthetic Hand

Designing a prosthetic hand that can be controlled with the user’s own mind. The project is really a coalescence of several advanced technologies, including 3D printed components, EEG brainwave sensing hardware, and mobile single-board computers. This project at aims to produce a functioning hand (excluding the EEG) for under a hundred dollars.

NOAA - Tracking the migration patterns of

Collaborated with NOAA biologists to capture, measure, tag, and study the threatened Hawaiian (Honu) green sea turtle. Taking place in Kiholo Bay, one of the prime habitats for juvenile green turtles on the west coast of Hawai'i, we studied the migratory behaviors of the Honu and Loggerhead turtles in the Pacific Ocean. Participated in 5+ field expeditions with NOAA Scientist and Director of the Sea Turtle Research Program, Marc Rice.
